### **1. Speaker 1 (Nguyen Gia Hung): Labor Market Trends and the Power of Foundations**
*   **Key Points:**
    *   **AI Paradox:** AI makes software development cheaper, leading to an explosion in software demand, which creates new jobs like fixing AI-generated code.
    *   **New Market Standards:** Beyond just demos, students must build real products that solve specific business problems (e.g., banking operations).
    *   **Enduring Value:** University degrees and core technical foundations remain vital for global competitiveness; AI is merely an amplifier for skilled engineers.

### **2. Speaker 2 (Tinh Truong): Context is Everything - Making AI Actually Work For You**
*   **Key Points:**
    *   **Internet Bullers:** A warning against "copy-pasting" code from the internet or AI without understanding the context, which leads to fragmented systems that don't fit company standards.
    *   **Context Optimization:** Provide domain-specific information to AI rather than generic knowledge available online to achieve the most accurate outputs.
    *   **AI Mindset & Adoption:** Learn how top-tier companies apply AI to their workflows instead of just using AI for school assignments.

### **3. Speaker 3 (Pham Nguyen Hai Anh): GenAI-Powered Auto Audit for AWS Workload**
*   **Key Points:**
    *   **Defining Agents:** Agent = Brain (LLM) + Extended Arms (Actions/Tools). Agents can perform tasks like scheduling and emailing, not just generating text.
    *   **Amazon QuickSight:** Enables non-technical users to create reports and analyze Excel data using natural language commands.

### **4. Speaker 4 (Nguyen Tuan Thinh): CloudFront as Your Foundation**
*   **Key Points:**
    *   **Flat-rate Pricing:** A new pricing mechanism that helps businesses avoid "bill spikes" caused by DDoS attacks or unexpected traffic surges.
    *   **High Performance:** Leverages AWS's internal fiber-optic network (Backbone) and HTTP/3 to reduce page load speeds by 81%.
    *   **Edge Security:** Introduces mTLS (Mutual TLS) for high-security financial apps and VPC Origin to completely hide infrastructure from the public internet.

### **5. Speaker 5 (UTMorpho): Building UTMorpho from Idea to Reality - 36hrs with LotusHacks**
*   **Key Points:**
    *   **UTMorpho Project:** An AI-powered UI editor that allows direct interaction with the interface instead of regenerating from scratch.
    *   **Multi-Agent Architecture:** Utilizes 3 specialized agents: Vision (image analysis), Layout (skeleton design), and Design (code generation).
    *   **Practical Lessons:** Managing token limits, controlling "AI over-generation," and the necessity of focusing on the Minimum Viable Product (MVP).

### **6. Speaker 6 (Duc Dao): Non-Determinism of "Deterministic" LLM Settings**
*   **Key Points:**
    *   **Probabilistic Nature:** LLMs are engines that predict the next token based on probabilities (Logits).
    *   **Optimization Bias:** Providers (OpenAI, AWS) use prompt-batching to optimize GPUs, causing variations in output even with Temperature set to 0.
    *   **Strategy:** Use JSON Mode, consider local hosting for absolute control, and always design downstream services to handle unexpected AI responses.

### **7. Speaker 7 (Vy Lam): Enterprise-Grade Multi-Agent System**
*   **Key Points:**
    *   **Solving Real Problems:** Startups often lack a 3-year financial history; AI systems can collect multi-dimensional data (market, team, IP) for alternative assessment.
    *   **Guardrails & Compliance:** Security is paramount in enterprises. Systems must define "boundaries" for agents and maintain audit trails to track every AI decision.
